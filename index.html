About Me

Hi there! I'm Ben, a graduate from the Geospatial Data Analytics program at the Centre of Geographic Sciences in Lawrencetown, Nova Scotia. This is a portfolio page I have setup through GitHub Pages, wherein I will showcase some of the skills I developed over the past school term.

I began my career a while ago in advertising and media planning. The first code I ever wrote was an Excel macro that helped me save time while inputting data from television commercial schedules. I kept looking for other ways to improve or automate technical processes to make my job easier, and along the way learned to cobble together 'spaghetti' code in a handful of different languages.

My experience at COGS was the first actual schooling I ever received in programming, and I was very happy with the experience. The thing I valued the most was that my instructors took care to contextualize new concepts and tools rather than just show us how to use them. For example, were were introduced to the Open Geospatial Constortium whose standards enable some of the most commonly spatial data formats like KMLs, GeoTiffs, and Geopackages. I was also happy to learn about things like style guidelines and how to implement test cases -- skills that are essential to code well with others, which is something I had never really needed to do before.

Of course I learned more at COGS than just how to use Python. Earlier classes in geodesy, remote sensing, database management and the use of GIS software provided foundational knowledge that... Spatial analysis, modelling, machine learning & neural networks. .
